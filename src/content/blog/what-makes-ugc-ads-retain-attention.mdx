## Native delivery is only the starting point

Many UGC ads look native enough to stop the scroll, but that alone does not sustain attention. The ad still needs a believable reason to keep going.

If the viewer feels the setup is too staged, too slow, or too detached from a real use case, the performance usually drops even if the creator presentation feels familiar.

## Proof must arrive before fatigue

UGC works best when the proof arrives early enough to reward curiosity. That proof can be a demo, a visual before-and-after, a specific claim, or a grounded user outcome.

When the video delays proof too long, the ad starts to feel like filler even if the creator seems authentic.

## CTA transitions matter more than most teams think

One of the biggest mistakes in UGC ads is treating the CTA like a separate add-on. The close performs better when it feels like a natural continuation of the proof instead of a sudden switch in tone.

That is why retention analysis should cover the whole flow instead of only the opening seconds.
